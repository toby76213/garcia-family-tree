The Garcia Family

South Texas Ranching Dynasty

1740 – Present · Eight Generations on the Land

Eight Generations Camargo, Tamaulipas · Starr & Brooks Counties, TX 250,000+ Acres · Royal Land Grant 1740
✦     ✦     ✦

The Garcia family of South Texas stands among the great unsung stories of American ranching. For nearly three centuries — from the first Royal Land Grant of 1740 through the cattle yards of the Rio Grande valley today — they shaped the land, the law, and the culture of the border region. Esteban Garcia alone negotiated royalties for an entire generation of ranchers, desegregated public facilities a decade before the Civil Rights Act, and helped broker an international treaty that saved the livestock industry on both sides of the border. Their legacy endures in the landscape, the bloodlines of a registered breed, and six generations of working ranchers who never left the land their ancestors were granted.
